Research Project

Master's Thesis

AI-Mediated Social Media Moderation System

Sept 2022 - Present
NYCU AIMEX Lab | Master's Thesis Research
  • Designed and developed a web-based HCI experiment platform to evaluate AI-assisted moderation interfaces for mitigating personal attacks in social media comment sections.
  • Implemented receiver-side browsing workflows and sender-side comment composition workflows across Label, Paraphrase, and Meme-based moderation conditions.
  • Built interaction logging pipelines to capture comment exposure, original-text reveal actions, user interface events, questionnaire responses, and post-task behavioral data.
  • Engineered Selenium-based crawlers and preprocessing scripts to collect realistic discussion data from Facebook, PTT, and Dcard for experimental scenario construction.
  • Integrated LLM-powered comment transformation workflows to convert hostile comments into safer paraphrased or meme-based representations while preserving optional access to original text.
  • Analyzed 112-participant user-study data with ordinal mixed-effects models in R to evaluate interface effects on perceived attack, negativity reduction, comment atmosphere, and browsing willingness.
  • Conducted semi-structured interviews and qualitative coding to identify design risks including over-filtering, hidden valuable criticism, moderation strictness, and user circumvention behavior.
